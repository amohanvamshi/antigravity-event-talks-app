1. U.S.-Iran Ceasefire Agreement
   - Pakistani Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif announced a peace agreement between the United States and Iran.
   - The deal includes an immediate and permanent end to military operations on all fronts, including Lebanon.
   - An official signing ceremony is expected to take place in Switzerland on June 19.
   - The agreement aims to reopen the Strait of Hormuz for global oil and natural gas shipments.
   - However, reports indicate confusion and diverging interpretations between U.S. and Iranian sources regarding the management of the strait, as Iran appears to view an "open" strait as one under its own management.
   - President Donald Trump told The New York Times that he would resume military attacks if Iran fails to reach a final nuclear agreement. He suggested a potential role for the U.S. as a "guardian of the Middle East" in exchange for a portion of the region's revenues.
   - Despite the ceasefire announcement, Israeli officials indicated that the Israel Defense Forces (IDF) would continue operations in Lebanon to degrade Hezbollah, while Hezbollah signaled it would adhere to the ceasefire provided it leads to an Israeli withdrawal.
